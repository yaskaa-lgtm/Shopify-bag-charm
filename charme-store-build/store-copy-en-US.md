## 3. OUR STORY

### Short (≈150 words)
Charmé started with a simple frustration: the bag we loved most had started to feel a little… done. Not worn out — just familiar. The fix wasn't a new bag. It was one small, well-made thing clipped to the old one.

So we went looking for a charm that actually felt like something — real enamel, solid metal, a clasp that grips and stays. Most of what we found was hollow and forgettable. So we made our own: small enough to live on any bag, substantial enough to notice, and finished to photograph like it cost a lot more than it does.

That's the whole idea. Charmé isn't about replacing what you love. It's about making it feel new again — one clip at a time. No reinvention required. Just a little charm, and a bag that suddenly feels like yours all over again.

> **Objective:** believable brand rationale, no fake heritage. **Objection handled:** "who are these people / why should I trust the taste level." Honest — no invented family origin story (FTC + brand voice).

### Long (≈300 words)
Charmé started with a bag that had stopped feeling exciting. It wasn't old or broken — it was just the bag, the one we'd carried so many times it had gone invisible. Buying a whole new one felt like overkill for what was really a small itch: we wanted it to feel new again, not to replace it.

The obvious fix was a bag charm. The problem was that almost every charm we could find felt like an afterthought — hollow, rattly, plasticky, the kind of thing that looks fine in a photo and disappointing in your hand. We wanted the opposite: something with a little weight to it, a finish that held up, and a clasp that actually stayed clipped through a full day of being tossed around.

So we made it. The Charmé Charm is built on a solid metal body with hand-set enamel and a wide-mouth lobster clasp. It's designed to move — from a structured work tote in the morning to a smaller bag at night — and to read beautifully on camera, because let's be honest, part of the joy is how it looks in the photo.

We kept the idea deliberately small. One well-made charm, a handful of designs, and a price that stays under thirty dollars so it can be an impulse for you or a genuinely good gift for someone else. We ship from a US warehouse, we tell you exactly when it'll arrive, and we pack it so it feels like a gift the second it lands.

That's Charmé. Not a reinvention of your style — just the small, considered detail that makes the bag you already love feel like yours all over again.

> **Objective:** deepen trust for high-intent readers + retargeting. **Objection handled:** quality standard, shipping honesty, gift positioning — all without fabricated backstory.
