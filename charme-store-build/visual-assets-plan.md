# D. Visual Assets Plan — Charmé

Style system for every frame: **cream `#F6F1E7` background, macro on textured leather, golden-hour light, one pop color per frame, hand-holding-the-bag where relevant, ZERO white catalog background.** Product shots must be real; environment/lifestyle can be AI or styled photography. Each asset lists role, ratio, px, max weight, SEO alt, and a ready-to-use prompt.

**Weight budget:** hero/LCP images ≤ 200KB (WebP), gallery ≤ 150KB, thumbnails ≤ 60KB. Export **WebP** (Shopify serves it). Always upload at the exact px below to avoid layout shift.

---

## Real-photo vs AI — read this first

| Must be a REAL photo of the actual product | Can be AI-generated or composited |
|---|---|
| PDP gallery image 1 (hero product) | Home hero lifestyle scene (bag + charm in an environment) |
| Scale shot (charm on a real bag) | Q4 gifting mood banner (background/props) |
| Packaging/unboxing shot | Announcement-bar micro-textures, section dividers |
| Any close macro used to prove enamel/metal quality | Social-proof background fields / color washes |
| At least 2 review-worthy detail shots | "How it works" step illustrations/icons |

**Why:** FTC + trust. If the customer receives something that doesn't match an AI "product" render, that's a deceptive-imagery and returns problem. **AI is for mood and environment; the product itself is always the real CJ item.** Get CJ product samples photographed (phone + natural light is fine) before launch. `⚠️ assumption: you can get 1 sample shot before D9 — if not, use CJ supplier images temporarily but replace with real shots within the first week.`

## Ready-to-use image prompts

Global style suffix to append to every prompt:
> *"...cream #F6F1E7 seamless background, soft golden-hour directional light, shallow depth of field, one saturated pop color, textured leather and warm neutrals, editorial DTC product photography, no text, no logos, no white studio background, photorealistic, high detail."*

**P1 — Hero product on cream** (real photo direction, or AI mock for layout only)
> "A single elevated enamel-and-metal bag charm resting on a cream surface, macro, the charm catching warm light on its metal clasp and glossy enamel, minimal styling, one pop of [persimmon/patina/gold] color, premium and tactile."

**P2 — Macro on leather**
> "Extreme close-up of an enamel-and-metal bag charm and its lobster clasp lying on richly textured tan leather, golden-hour light raking across the surface, shallow depth of field emphasizing the enamel gloss and metal weight."

**P3 — Scale on a bag**
> "An enamel bag charm clipped onto the handle of a structured leather tote, shot to show real-world size, cream and caramel tones, one pop of color from the charm, natural window light."

**P4 — Gifting/packaging**
> "An enamel bag charm beside minimalist gift-ready packaging in cream and soft gold, tissue and a slim box, warm light, premium unboxing feel."

**P5 — Lifestyle in-hand**
> "A woman's hand holding an investment leather handbag with an enamel charm clipped to the strap, golden-hour city light, cream and warm tones, aspirational and candid, focus on the charm."

**H1 — Home hero**
> "A handbag styled on a warm neutral surface with an enamel bag charm as the hero detail, golden-hour light, cream palette, one pop of color, editorial and aspirational, generous negative space on the [left/top] for headline text."

**H4 — Q4 gifting banner**
> "Three enamel bag charms arranged as a gift set with subtle seasonal styling (warm candlelight, soft evergreen hint) on cream, gold accents, premium and cozy, negative space for a headline."

**H3 — How-it-works steps** (3 prompts)
> Step 1: "Hands clipping an enamel charm onto a bag strap, cream background, close and clear, instructional but beautiful."
> Step 2: "A styled bag with the charm adding one pop of color, cream background, showing the finished look."
> Step 3: "The same charm being moved to a different, smaller bag, cream background, showing versatility."

## Production notes
- Generate 3–5 variations per hero frame; pick on a 390px screen, not desktop.
- Keep **one** pop color per frame — mixing colors kills the premium read.
- Compress via Shopify's auto-WebP; double-check final weights against the budget (verify in G / PageSpeed).
- Replace any temporary supplier imagery with real product shots within week 1 (FTC + returns).
